### Cybersecurity Bootcamp Capstone &nbsp;|&nbsp; September 2025

## Objective

Perform a full cybersecurity risk assessment on Republic Bank (Barbados) Limited based on OSINT findings — identifying organisational vulnerabilities, scoring them by likelihood and impact, and developing actionable recommendations using industry-standard frameworks.

* * *

## What I Did

Using findings from the OSINT capstone as the foundation, I conducted a structured risk assessment covering vulnerability identification, likelihood and impact scoring, asset-based analysis, and framework application.

* * *

## Asset-Based Summary

**Asset 1 — Corporate Governance Infrastructure**
- Classification: Critical Organisational Process Asset
- Severity: HIGH (Risk Score: 16)
- Frameworks: COBIT 2019, COSO, NIST CSF PR.AC

**Asset 2 — Regulatory Compliance Systems**
- Classification: Critical Information Technology Asset
- Severity: CRITICAL (Risk Score: 15)
- Frameworks: Central Bank of Barbados Banking Supervision Guidelines, TTSEC Framework, Basel Operational Risk Principles

* * *

## Recommendations

**Recommendation 1 — Strengthen Executive Protection**
- Implement advanced email security with zero-trust authentication and MFA for all senior executives
- Provide cybersecurity training focused on social engineering and spear-phishing recognition
- Establish secure travel protocols and encrypted channels for sensitive communications
- Deploy continuous monitoring to detect unauthorised use of executive information

**Recommendation 2 — Secure Regulatory Communications**
- Implement authentication protocols including digital signatures for all regulatory messages
- Establish mandatory verification procedures for any directives received from regulators
- Create direct threat-sharing channels with TTSEC and the Central Bank of Barbados
- Implement a compliance management system integrating policies, controls, and audit procedures

* * *

## Key Takeaway

Extensive public documentation creates measurable vulnerabilities. While eliminating such documentation is not feasible given regulatory transparency requirements, implementing robust defensive controls and monitoring significantly reduces organisational risk exposure.
