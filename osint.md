# OSINT Investigation
### Cybersecurity Bootcamp Capstone &nbsp;|&nbsp; August 2025

[Back to Portfolio](./index.html)

* * *

## Objective

Conduct a comprehensive Open-Source Intelligence investigation on a senior banking executive at Republic Bank Barbados using only publicly available sources — to identify what sensitive information was exposed online and how threat actors could exploit it.

* * *

## What I Did

I used three distinct OSINT methodologies to build a complete intelligence profile on the target:

**Methodology 1 — Corporate & Regulatory Database Mining**
Searched publicly accessible corporate databases, organisational charts, and regulatory filing systems including TTSEC documents and bank executive team listings. Examined corporate structures, board composition, and compliance documentation to understand reporting relationships and institutional connections.

**Methodology 2 — Cross-Platform Verification and Network Analysis**
Conducted systematic analysis across LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. Mapped professional connections to identify key relationships within the banking sector, government, and business community. Cross-referenced information across platforms to verify accuracy and identify inconsistencies.

**Methodology 3 — Search Engine Insights and Media Analysis**
Used targeted keyword searches across news media, corporate websites, and professional databases. Cross-referenced information across multiple Caribbean news outlets including Barbados Today, Guyana Chronicle, and Trinidad-based publications.

* * *

## Sensitive Information Identified

| Item | Category | Sensitivity Level |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Professional career timeline & employer history (25-year progression 1999–2024) | Career Intelligence | HIGH |
| Organisational structure — 38 named executives with roles and reporting lines | Corporate Intelligence | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| Public speaking engagements & community participation patterns | Operational Security | HIGH |
| Business direction, digital transformation strategy & market positioning | Strategic Intelligence | CRITICAL |
| Regulatory and compliance documentation across multiple jurisdictions | Compliance Intelligence | HIGH |

* * *

## Threat Actor Exploitation Analysis

**Exploitation 1 — Social Engineering & Insider Intelligence**
The detailed 25-year career progression could be used to craft convincing spear-phishing attacks impersonating former colleagues, referencing shared experiences and mutual contacts to establish false credibility.

**Exploitation 2 — Network Infiltration**
The publicly accessible organisational chart of 38 executives provides a complete attack surface map — identifying high-value targets, understanding reporting hierarchies for privilege escalation, and enabling business email compromise campaigns.

**Exploitation 3 — Physical and Operational Security**
Documentation of public speaking engagements and scheduled appearances creates patterns that threat actors could exploit for surveillance, social engineering at events, or timed attacks during predictable absences.

**Exploitation 4 — Market Manipulation and Strategic Sabotage**
Public statements on strategic priorities and digital transformation initiatives provide competitors or threat actors with intelligence that could be used for market manipulation or targeted attacks against specific technology implementations.

**Exploitation 5 — Regulatory Impersonation and Compliance Manipulation**
Publicly accessible regulatory documentation enables sophisticated impersonation schemes — crafting fraudulent regulatory communications that appear legitimate to both the bank and its customers.

* * *

## Key Takeaway

Publicly available information can be weaponised. Understanding what an organisation exposes online is the first step in reducing its attack surface. This investigation demonstrated that a determined threat actor could build a highly credible profile using only open sources — without accessing a single internal system.
